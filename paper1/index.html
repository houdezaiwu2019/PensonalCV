paper1

Online prediction of dynamical characteristics of thin-walled workpiece, such as turbine blades, during the material removal, plays an important role in constructing digital twins system for high-performance machining processes. However, the complex surfaces, thin-walled structures as well as the time-varying characteristics of blade machining process bring great challenge. The existing methods are either for simple structure or unadaptable to the continuous varying of the modal parameters, which cannot meet the requirements of online prediction for complex blade machining. To this end, this paper constructs a mode shape generative adversarial network (GAN) and a frequency regression network(FRN). By taking the geometric information as input, online prediction of the mode shape and natural frequency during the machining process of thin-walled blades are realized, where the networks are trained based on the simulation data obtained by finite element method (FEM) and matrix perturbation method. Considering the shift of machining parameters, for example, depth of cut varies due to machining deformation, which inevitably brings deviation of the measured and predicted frequency in machining process, a Xgboost model is established with the shared features of the FRN to modified the original frequency regression network(FRN), which enables the model be adaptive to machining uncertainties. By integrating the proposed network into the self-developed CAM software, a digital shadow system of dynamic parameters during blade machining is constructed. The verification experiments show that the above system can achieve high-performance online prediction of modal parameters in thin-walled complex blade machining process.
